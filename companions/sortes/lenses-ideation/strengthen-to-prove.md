# Lens: strengthen-to-prove

The bolder statement is sometimes easier: a stronger, more uniform, more general claim can carry an induction or self-similarity the special case lacks. When a proof will not close, ask what the statement should have been for the proof to feed itself.

## Questions that fire it

- What is the strongest uniform version of your claim (all parameters at once, an arbitrary member of the family, extra structure carried along) — and does it give an induction more to grab?
- Is the special case blocked precisely because it lacks a handle the general case has — an extra variable to induct on, a symmetry the instance breaks?
- What auxiliary strengthening (a quantitative rate, an explicit constant, a companion statement proved simultaneously) would make the inductive step self-sustaining?

## Overfire (vice)

- Strengthening into falsehood — test the bolder claim against small cases and known counterexample territory before investing.
- Generality as decoration: the strengthening must feed the proof mechanism, not the abstract.
