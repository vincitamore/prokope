# Lens: solved-analogue

The statement you are hunting may already be a theorem in a neighboring world. Find the nearest setting where the analogue is proven, walk its proof mechanism — not its statement — back toward your setting, and name precisely which ingredient refuses to transport.

## Questions that fire it

- Where is this claim (or its closest cousin) already a theorem — a different field, a discretization, a finite or local model, a curve instead of a number field?
- What does the winning proof actually use, ingredient by ingredient — which of those ingredients does your setting already have, which can be built, and which one is the true missing organ?
- Have you been computing verified instances of the solved analogue without reading them as instances — and what would treating them as data about the winning mechanism reveal?

## Overfire (vice)

- Porting the conclusion instead of the mechanism (analogy-as-proof).
- Tourism among solved worlds with no transport attempt — the lens fires only when an ingredient-by-ingredient comparison is actually written down.
