# Lens: deform-to-solvable

Embed the stuck instance in a continuous family with a control parameter whose other endpoint is solved or trivial. Then study the path, not the endpoints: where along the family does the property break, and what changes at the breaking point?

## Questions that fire it

- What one-parameter family contains both your problem and a solvable or trivial degeneration of it — a deformation, a truncation, a weighting, a generalized model with your case as a limit?
- Is the property you want monotone, continuous, or abruptly lost along the path — and what is the first parameter value where the proof for the easy endpoint stops working?
- Can the breaking point itself become the object of study (a phase boundary, a critical constant, a transition), turning "unknown" into "locate a threshold"?

## Overfire (vice)

- Families with no solvable endpoint (deformation for its own sake).
- Treating behavior at the easy endpoint as evidence about the far endpoint without tracking what the path does in between.
