# Lens: extremal-reframe

Turn the question into an optimization: what object extremizes the relevant quantity under the natural constraints? The extremizer's first-order characterization — orthogonality, equality cases, stationarity — often exposes machinery the original phrasing hides.

## Questions that fire it

- What quantity is your claim secretly a bound on — and what is the exact extremal problem (sup or inf, over what class, under what normalization)?
- What must the extremizers satisfy by necessity (stationarity, equality in the inequality you used, complementary constraints) — and do those conditions identify them as familiar objects?
- Is there a dual formulation whose optimal value meets yours — and does duality convert your one-sided bound into an equivalence?

## Overfire (vice)

- Optimizing proxies that do not bound the target — the reframe must be exact, or one-sided with the side named.
- Numerical optimization presented as characterization — the extremizer's structure, not its decimal expansion, is the product.
