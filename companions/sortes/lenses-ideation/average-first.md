# Lens: average-first

Before the single hard instance, prove the statement on average over the natural family it lives in — in the mean, for almost all members, in low moments. Averages are provable where instances are not, and an almost-all theorem is real claimed territory that also localizes where exceptions could hide.

## Questions that fire it

- What is the natural family containing your instance (a parameter aspect, a range of moduli, a sibling ensemble), and what does the target look like averaged over it?
- Can you compute the first and second moments of the relevant quantity over the family — and does variance control already give "true for almost all"?
- What would the exceptions have to look like for the average to hold while an instance fails — does the exceptional set have exploitable structure of its own?

## Overfire (vice)

- Averages over convenience families with no route back to the instance.
- Quietly upgrading "almost all" to "all" in downstream language — keep the warrant scale honest.
