# Lens: local-global-glue

Study the object one place at a time — one modulus, one chart, one scale, one component, one locale — then ask what reassembles the local pictures into the global object, and what obstructs the gluing. The obstruction to reassembly is often the deep content.

## Questions that fire it

- What natural “places” or localizations does the problem admit, and what is known or easy at each place in isolation?
- What is the gluing data (compatibility conditions, cocycle, transition maps) — and which local solutions refuse to glue?
- Is the obstruction to gluing structural (a cohomology class, a monodromy, a consistency failure) or merely computational — and does that change what you should prove next?
