# Lens: equivalent-forms

A hard target usually has many provably equivalent formulations, and each exposes different tools. Shop the equivalence class: restate the target in the form where your substrate has the most native leverage, and attack that form.

## Questions that fire it

- What known statements are provably equivalent to your target (not merely related) — and which of them is phrased in objects your substrate already computes well?
- Does some equivalent form convert the target's hardest ingredient into one your tools natively handle (an inequality, a density, a growth rate, a positivity, a fixed-point, a type-check)?
- If no catalogued equivalent fits, can you manufacture one — prove a new equivalence to a form you can at least attack numerically or formally at scale?

## Overfire (vice)

- Form-shopping as procrastination: the tour of equivalents must end with an attack on one of them.
- Treating near-equivalents as equivalents — one lost epsilon in the equivalence proof and the attack proves something else.
