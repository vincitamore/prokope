# Lens: work-backward

Assume the target is true and walk the implications downstream until you reach the weakest claim you might prove independently — then attack that link from below. The corridor from the summit down meets the ground somewhere; find the meeting point.

## Questions that fire it

- If the target held, what is the first genuinely NEW checkable consequence — one not already known unconditionally?
- Along the implication chain from target to known ground, which single link is weakest — and can that link be proved outright, or at least attacked with existing tools?
- Does the backward walk reveal what the proof's final step must look like — and what objects must exist for that step to make sense?

## Overfire (vice)

- Conditional towers with no attempt to ground any link (assuming the target forever is not a method).
- Mistaking "consistent with the target" for "consequence of the target" — the corridor must be implications, not resonances.
