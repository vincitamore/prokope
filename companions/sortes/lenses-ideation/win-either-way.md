# Lens: win-either-way

Split on the unknown hypothesis and work both branches: consequences of truth, consequences of a specific structured failure. Where the branches overlap, the overlap is unconditional — uncertainty itself becomes a proof engine.

## Questions that fire it

- What follows if the target is true? What follows — often surprisingly strong — if it fails in a specific structured way (a single bad witness, a minimal violation)?
- Do any consequences hold on BOTH branches, yielding an unconditional theorem from an undecided hypothesis?
- Can the failure branch be developed as a rich world in its own right, so that either that world is real or the obstacles to building it are informative?

## Overfire (vice)

- Case splits where one branch is never actually developed.
- Disjunction inflation: "A or B" pays only when the overlap or the disjunction itself is new content.
