# Lens: find-the-action

Before computing with an object, find what acts on it — a group, a flow, an operator family — and decompose the problem along that action. A method's power zone is its generator's eigenspace: match the transform to the symmetry the object actually carries, and the pieces become simpler than the whole.

## Questions that fire it

- What transformations leave your object (or its defining property) invariant or covariant — translations, dilations, inversions, permutations of factors — and which have you never exploited?
- What does the problem look like decomposed along that action's eigenfunctions, irreducibles, or orbits — which components carry the difficulty and which trivialize?
- If your current transform keeps failing, is the object simply not built from that generator's eigenfunctions — and what generator IS it built from?

## Overfire (vice)

- Symmetry tourism: naming actions without decomposing anything.
- Forcing a favorite group onto an object that carries a different one — the mismatch is precisely the blindness to name.
