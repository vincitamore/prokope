# Lens: obstruction-anatomy

When you prove something cannot be reached, the proof produces a certificate — a witness, a dual object, a conserved quantity. Study the space of those certificates as a first-class object: the wall is built from the same substance as the thing it hides.

## Questions that fire it

- What exactly certifies the failure — a witness function, a dual vector, an invariant — and what is the full space of such certificates, not just the one you found?
- What structure does the certificate space carry (dimension, symmetry, extremal members, a kernel), and what known objects does it secretly parametrize?
- If the obstruction weakened or vanished at some parameter value, what would the certificates degenerate into — and does that degeneration point at the mechanism controlling the original question?

## Overfire (vice)

- Re-deriving the obstruction in new coordinates and calling the relabel anatomy (that is transport, not structure).
- Cataloging certificates with no question they answer — the anatomy must aim at what the wall is made of, not at a museum of walls.
