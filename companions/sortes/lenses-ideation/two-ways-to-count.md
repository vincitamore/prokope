# Lens: two-ways-to-count

One quantity, two computations — an identity is born where two structurally different counting procedures meet the same total. Build the object that both sides must equal; the bridge you need is often an accounting identity waiting to be noticed.

## Questions that fire it

- What quantity in your setting can be computed two structurally different ways — by position and by frequency, by orbit and by stabilizer, spectrally and geometrically, term-by-term and by generating function?
- If your two mysteries sit on opposite sides of some ledger, what single total would force them to balance — and what family of test inputs turns that balance into usable inequalities?
- Where a known two-sided identity exists nearby, what does YOUR object contribute to one side, and what does the other side then say back?

## Overfire (vice)

- Identities verified numerically and left as packaging — the lens pays only when a two-sided identity is proved and then worked (specialized, tested against extremes).
- Rebuilding a classical identity in new coordinates and counting it as a bridge (that is transport).
