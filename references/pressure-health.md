# Pressure health — reference

**The problem.** Long goal loops can keep **claim honesty** (no false summit, every claim scoped) while **search pressure** dies: no load-bearing falsifications, frozen work templates, silent trails. The two axes fail independently and must be instrumented separately.

**The characteristic failure signature** of a long unattended loop — the axes decay in a recognizable order:

- Claim honesty holds: every status entry still says the target is open; nothing is fabricated or oversold.
- The falsification rate decays with context depth — early work kills freely, late work never dies, and the final stretch contains no live kills at all.
- Draw-tagging and trail hygiene lapse mid-marathon — often exactly the stretch producing the loop's most substantial artifacts, leaving them unattributable to any draw.
- Late iterations converge on a fixed outcome template that repeats regardless of which lenses were drawn: the *draw* stays varied while the *shape of the work* does not.

**Operational rule:** re-arm by session restart plus goal-brief re-post when pressure is sick **or** post-boundary orientation fails (tip lag, standing-block amnesia, a summary claiming altitude the tips deny). Continuity lives in the search log, the status tip, and version control — never in one marathon context window or a compaction summary. A fresh context restores kill pressure, tag discipline, and genuine draw-following, provided the surfaces carry the state.

**Signals to instrument in briefs:**

1. Time (or iterations) since the last **load-bearing** kill or demotion
2. Multi-arm waves missing draw tags or `not surfaced:` trails
3. Draw diversity without outcome-shape diversity
4. Duration of adversarial-review silence
5. Length of the current engine-class re-skin streak
6. **Tip freshness across boundaries** (search-log tip ↔ status tip agree; written before the last compaction)

Prefer signal triggers over fixed hour or iteration quotas — the right cadence is a property of the goal, set in its brief.
